Must See's in Richmond

Richmond provide's many fun and beautiful must see attractions. Prided on it's historical pressence it is home to many great museums and tours.Additionally Richmond is home to many parks, golf courses and recreational activities.

Museums and Historical attractions

Irviton House Park and Museum

Irvinton House

Locaated just outside the heart of dowtown Richmond is the Irvinton House and Park. Left to the City after the death of Elizabeth Irvine she stipulated that all furniture and belongings be left and the home to be left to the medical society and was used as a hostpital for many years.Now functioning as a city park and the home is about for tours.

White Hall State Historic Site

White Hall Historic Site State Park

The White Hall State Historic site is nestled on rolling acres of farm land just on the edge of madison county. White Hall is the home that was in the Clay politcal family for many generations. The home was the first of many to have modern ammnenties for the time such as an indoor pluming system and indoor heating. Now under the managment of Eastern Kentucky University, tours are avalible of the home and the property offers many walking trails as well as playground and pond.

Battle of Richmond Civil War Battle War Battle Grounds

Battle of Richmond Civil War Battle Grounds

The Battle of Richmond,KY was fought on August Twenty nineth and Thirthy of 1862. The Battle grounds are located at 101 Battlefield Memorial Highway and are free to tour to guest. The grounds contain many walking trails and a musuem.

Arlington "The University Club" Country Club

Donated to Eastern Kentucky University in 1967, The Arlington Estate is comprised of over 166 acres of farmland that was additionly bought by the university. The modern country club includes ammenties such as a beautiful 18 hole course, olympic sized swimming pool and tennis courts. The property also includes a club house with a resturant, Mansion that holds social events as well as a smaller venue known as the "Mule Barn".

Mill Stone Park

Located in the heart of downtown Richmond, Millstone Park provides a history of the milling history in Richmond. The Park provides a walk through of many mills and a mural. The first anuanl Millstone festival was hosted in downtown Richmond to celebrate the history of Richmond and bring the community together.
